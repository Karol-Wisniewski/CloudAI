Polish poet and publicist of the Romanticism period (leading of the trinity of “wieszczów”). The son of a lawyer, Nicholas (d. 1812) of the Poraj coat of arms and Barbara of Majewskie. He graduated from the Faculty of Literature of Vilnius University; he worked his scholarship as a teacher in Kaunas. He was a co-founder of the secret self-learning Philomath Society (1817), for which he was arrested and sentenced to settle in the hinterlands of Russia in 1823. From 1824 to 1829 he stayed in Saint Petersburg, Moscow and the Crimea; then in exile in Paris. He taught Latin literature at the Academy of Lausanne (1839) and, from 1840, Slavic literature at the College de France in Paris. In 1841, he became associated with the religious movement of A. Toiański. During the Spring of the Ludów period, he was the editor-in-chief of the daily “Trybuna Ludów” and organizer of the Polish Volunteer Deputy, for which he wrote a democratic oneSkład zasad.
